# &#x09;				Cybersecurity









* ###### **Cybersecurity** is the practice of protecting computers, networks, programs, and data from unauthorized access, attacks, damage, or theft.



* ###### In short, **cybersecurity** is like digital self-defense: keeping your data, systems, and networks safe from attackers.



* ###### Goal: Ensure Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability (CIA triad).









* ##### **CIA Triad :-**



###### **Term			Meaning**



###### Confidentiality		Only authorized access to data

###### Integrity		Data is accurate and untampered

###### Availability		Data \& systems are accessible when needed











* ##### **Why Cybersecurity is Important ?**





1. Prevent data breaches and identity theft

   ---
2. Protect sensitive information (personal, financial, or business data)

   ---
3. Prevent cyber attacks like hacking, phishing, ransomware

   ---
4. ###### Maintain trust in digital services and systems













* ##### **Main Areas of Cybersecurity**

###### 

###### 

###### **Area							Description**

###### 

###### **Network** **Security**					Protects networks from unauthorized access or attacks

###### **Application Security**					Secures software applications against vulnerabilities

###### **Information** **Security**					Protects data (at rest, in transit, or in use)

###### **Endpoint** **Security**					Secures devices like laptops, phones, and servers

###### **Cloud** **Security**						Protects data and apps hosted in cloud environments

###### **Identity \&** **Access** **Management (IAM)**			Ensures only authorized users can access resources

###### **Ethical Hacking / Penetration Testing**			Tests systems to find vulnerabilities before attackers do









* #### **Common Cyber Threats :-**



1. **Malware:** 				Viruses, worms, ransomware

   ---
2. **Phishing:** 				Fake emails or messages to steal data

   ---
3. **DDoS attacks:** 			Overloading a server/network to cause disruption

   ---
4. **Man-in-the-middle attacks:** 		Intercepting communication

   ---
5. ###### **Password attacks:** 			Brute force, credential stuffing
